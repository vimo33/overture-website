About Overture

Local culture should be easier to support, fairer to build, and more visible in everyday city life.

We started Overture with a simple belief: the systems around local culture should reflect the value culture creates for a city.

Our story

What if supporting local culture was structured, recurring, and visible?

Too much of local live culture depends on fragmented effort. Artists do invisible labor just to be considered. Venues carry risk without enough support. Audiences care, but often have no simple, trustworthy way to channel that care into something tangible.

Overture began as a response to that gap. What if the same system that helped audiences discover concerts also helped artists get paid and helped venues come alive? That question became Overture.

What we are building

Overture is a subscriber-backed cultural infrastructure for local live music.

  • Audiences support live culture through recurring participation
  • Artists access paid opportunities through a more transparent system
  • Venues become part of a coordinated cultural network
  • Money, attention, and opportunity move more visibly
What we believe

Five principles for a fairer local scene.

1. Culture is not a luxury add-on

It shapes how a city feels, how people gather, and how local identity survives.

2. Fairness needs structure

If we want more just outcomes for artists and venues, goodwill alone is not enough.

3. Transparency builds trust

People are more willing to support something when they can understand how it works.

4. Small rooms matter

Not every important cultural experience happens on a big stage.

5. Community is part of the infrastructure

A scene is built by artists, venues, and the people who actually show up.

What makes Overture different

Not another generic marketplace. A local system.

Local first Transparent by design Artist-aware Venue-aware Community-rooted Built for repeat participation
