## Intro
Being an independent artist often means doing far more than making the work.  
You write emails. You follow up. You wait. You guess.

We believe the system should not be this opaque.

Overture is our attempt to build that bridge.

## Section: What Overture offers artists
### A more transparent route into live performance
We are creating a city-level platform where subscriber support helps fund live shows across partner venues. Artists are paid directly through the platform, and programming is guided by clear rules, local fit, and curatorial intention.

### What that means for you
**Paid opportunities**  
We aim to create recurring, fairly structured performance opportunities connected to real audience demand.

**Clearer selection logic**  
Not a black box. Not endless uncertainty. We want artists to understand how opportunities are created and how decisions are made.

**Direct payout**  
The platform is designed so artist payment is central, visible, and intentional.

**A profile in a living ecosystem**  
Overture is not just about one performance. It is about becoming discoverable inside a local cultural network.

---

## Section: What we value
### We are not chasing polished sameness
We care about local relevance, artistic integrity, and building a city where more voices have space to be heard.

We are interested in artists who bring:
- distinct artistic identity
- emotional presence
- commitment to live performance
- openness to community and collaboration
- resonance with intimate or local formats

We know not every artist fits every room.  
That is why matching matters.

---

## Section: How artists are considered
### Fairness needs structure
Our goal is not to promise that every artist is booked equally.  
Our goal is to build a system that is more transparent, more intentional, and less arbitrary than what many artists experience today.

Programming decisions may consider:
- artistic quality and live readiness
- local fit
- format fit with available venues
- subscriber/community resonance
- rotation and diversity across the platform
- balance between emerging and more established artists

As Overture evolves, we want these principles to remain visible, not hidden behind vague language.

---

## Section: Payment philosophy
### We believe artists should not be the last priority in the budget
Too often, artists absorb the instability of the whole system.  
We are designing Overture so that artist compensation is not an afterthought but one of the core reasons the model exists.

Our aim is to:
- make payouts transparent
- align with fairer compensation principles where possible
- reduce the culture of unpaid labor and guesswork
- improve predictability over time

We are still building the model carefully, and we would rather be honest about that than make promises we cannot keep.

# About Page

## Hero
**We started Overture with a simple belief: local culture should be easier to support, fairer to build, and more visible in everyday city life.**

---

## Our story
Too much of local live culture depends on fragmented effort.  
Artists do invisible labor just to be considered.  
Venues carry risk without enough support.  
Audiences care, but often have no simple, trustworthy way to channel that care into something tangible.

Overture began as a response to that gap.

We asked a basic question:  
What if supporting local culture was not occasional, vague, or purely symbolic — but structured, recurring, and visible?

What if the same system that helped audiences discover concerts also helped artists get paid and helped venues come alive?

That question became Overture.

---

## What we are building
Overture is a subscriber-backed cultural infrastructure for local live music.

We are building a platform where:
- audiences support live culture through recurring participation
- artists access paid opportunities through a more transparent system
- venues become part of a coordinated cultural network
- the movement of money, attention, and opportunity becomes more visible

We are starting in Zurich because local ecosystems matter.  
Culture is always experienced somewhere specific first.

---

## What we believe
### 1. Culture is not a luxury add-on
It shapes how a city feels, how people gather, and how local identity survives.

### 2. Fairness needs structure
If we want more just outcomes for artists and venues, goodwill alone is not enough.

### 3. Transparency builds trust
People are more willing to support something when they can understand how it works.

### 4. Small rooms matter
Not every important cultural experience happens on a big stage.

### 5. Community is part of the infrastructure
A scene is not only built by artists and venues. It is built by people who show up.

---

## What makes Overture different
We are not trying to become another generic music marketplace.  
We are trying to create a local system where cultural support is easier to sustain and more directly connected to lived experience.

That means:
- local first
- transparent by design
- artist-aware
- venue-aware
- community-rooted
- built for repeat cultural participation, not one-off transactions alone

---

## Closing
Overture is still being built.  
But the need is already here.

If local culture matters, we believe the systems around it should reflect that — with care, clarity, and the courage to try something better.
